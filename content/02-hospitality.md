## Grammar notes

### The "X laagbe" pattern (to need)
`laagbe` literally means "will be needed." The thing needed goes first, then `laagbe`:

- `shomoy laagbe` = (I) need time
- `ak-ta gari laagbe` = (we) need a car
- `kichhu laagbe?` = do (you) need anything?

To ask in the past/present tense ("did/do you need"): replace `laagbe` with `legechhe` or `lagchhe`.

### "Khaben" — eat AND drink
In Bangla, the verb `khawa` (to eat) covers both eating *and* drinking. `Cha khaben?` literally is "Will you eat tea?" — but it means "Will you have tea?"

### Offering politely
The formal request form ends in **-en** or **-nin**:
- `boshen` = please sit
- `khaben` = will you eat / please eat
- `nin` = please take
- `bishram nin` = please rest
