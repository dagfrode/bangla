## Words
- poribar = family
- ma = mother
- baba = father
- bhai = brother
- bon = sister
- chhele = son / boy
- meye = daughter / girl
- shami = husband
- stree = wife
- shoshur = father-in-law
- shashuri = mother-in-law
- dada = paternal grandfather
- dadi = paternal grandmother
- nana = maternal grandfather
- nani = maternal grandmother
- chacha = paternal uncle (father's brother)
- chachi = chacha's wife
- fupu = paternal aunt (father's sister)
- mama = maternal uncle (mother's brother)
- mami = mama's wife
- khala = maternal aunt (mother's sister)
- bondhu = friend
- ini = this (person, formal — used about elders)
- uni = he/she (formal, about the person you respect)

## Phrases
- Eta amar poribar = This is my family
- Ini amar ma = This is my mother (formal — about your mother)
- Uni amar baba = He is my father (formal)
- Ini amar bhai = This is my brother
- Eta amar bon = This is my sister
- O amar bondhu = He/she is my friend (familiar — for friends your age)
- Apnar koy-jon chhele-meye? = How many children do you have?
- Amader poribar choto = Our family is small
- Norwayte amar poribar thaken = My family lives in Norway
- Apni amader poribar-er ongsho = You are part of our family

## Grammar notes

### "This is X" — pick the right pronoun
- For *objects*: **eta** (this is) — `eta amar basha` = this is my home
- For *people you respect*: **ini** (this is, formal) — `ini amar ma` = this is my mother
- For *people, familiar*: **e** or **she** — `e amar bondhu` = this is my friend

When introducing your Norwegian family to your in-laws, default to **ini** for the parents and elders, **e** for siblings your age or younger.

### Possessives
Add **-r** or **-er** to the person:
- `amar` = my (ami + r)
- `apnar` = your (apni + r)
- `tomar` = your (tumi + r)
- `tar` = his / her (ta + r)
- `amader` = our (amra + der)

So: `amar ma` = my mother. `apnar baba` = your father.

### "Children" combo word
`chhele-meye` (son-daughter) means *children* — Bangla often pairs the male+female words to make a category.
