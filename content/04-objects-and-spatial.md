## Grammar notes

### Position words follow the object
Unlike English ("on the table"), Bangla puts the position **after** the object, with the object in possessive form:

- `tabil-er upore` = on the table (lit. "table-of on")
- `bichana-r niche` = under the bed (lit. "bed-of under")
- `dorja-r pashe` = beside the door

The `-er` / `-r` is the possessive marker (same one as `amar`, `apnar`).

### "Where is X?" pattern
`X kothay?` literally is "X where?" — just put the thing first, then `kothay`. Add `achhe` for emphasis: `Chabi kothay achhe?` = "Where is the key (located)?"

### Locative case "-e" / "-y" / "-te"
For "in/at" a place, attach **-e** (or **-y** after a vowel, **-te** for some words):
- `basha-y` = at home
- `office-e` = at the office
- `gari-te` = in the car
- `Norway-te` = in Norway

(You saw `ami bashay` in the earlier notes — that's `basha + y`.)
