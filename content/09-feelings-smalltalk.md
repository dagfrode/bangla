- Amar bhalo lagchhe = I am feeling good (right now)

- Amar bhalo lagchhe na = I am not feeling well
- Amar khushi laagchhe = I am feeling happy
- Ami klanto = I am tired

## Grammar notes

### The "feelings" pattern: amar X laagchhe
Many feelings in Bangla are expressed as "to me, X is being felt." The grammatical *subject* is the feeling, and *you* are in the possessive:

- `amar bhalo laagchhe` = I am feeling good (lit. "to me, good is being felt")
- `amar khide laagchhe` = I am hungry (lit. "to me, hunger is being felt")
- `amar ghum laagchhe` = I am sleepy
- `amar thanda laagchhe` = I am feeling cold

Notice this is the same `laagbe`/`laagchhe` verb as "need" — context tells you which.

### Three tenses of "feel" — lagchhe vs legechhe vs laagbe
- `lagchhe` = is feeling (right now, present continuous)
- `legechhe` = felt (in the recent past, completed)
- `laagbe` = will feel / will need (future)

So `khabar kemon lagchhe?` (how is the food *now*) vs `khabar kemon legechhe?` (how was the food, asked after the meal).

### Weather "X porchhe"
Weather events use `pora` (to fall):
- `gorom porchhe` = it is getting hot (heat is falling)
- `rod porechhe` = sun has come out
- `thanda pore` = cold falls (habitual present)
