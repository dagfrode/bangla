## Grammar notes

### How to read this table
Each row is one verb. Columns:

- **Infinitive** — the dictionary form (ends in `-a` or `-no`)
- **English** — meaning
- **Present** — habitual: "you do" (apni)
- **Present continuous** — happening now: "you are doing"
- **Past** — completed: "you did"
- **Future** — "you will do"
- **Imperative** — request: "please do"

All forms are the **apni** (formal) form — what you use with in-laws.

### The infinitive in compound expressions
The infinitive in **-te** is used in "come to do" / "go to do" patterns:

- `khete ashen` = come to eat (khete = "to eat" + ashen = please come)
- `dekhte ashen` = come to see
- `bolte den` = let (me) speak (bolte = "to say" + den = give)

### Negation
Add **na** *after* the verb for present/past, and **na** at the end for imperative:

- `ami kori na` = I do not do
- `ami korchhi na` = I am not doing
- `apni korben na` = you will not do
- `koren na` = please do not do (imperative)

### The "is/am/have" verbs: achhi / achhen / achhe
- `ami achhi` = I am (present)
- `apni achhen` = you are (formal)
- `o / shey achhe` = he/she is
- `chhilen` = was (apni, past) → `apni kothay chhilen?` = where were you?
